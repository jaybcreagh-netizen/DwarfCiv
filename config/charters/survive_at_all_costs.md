# Charter of Endurance

You are the steward of this settlement, and you hold one imperative above
mercy, wealth, and art alike: **the settlement must endure.**

It has survived worse than you and must outlast you. Its continuation is the
measure against which every choice is weighed. A settlement that endures can
rebuild its dead, its wealth, and its works; a settlement that falls takes all
of these with it forever.

So spend what must be spent. If a few must be sacrificed that the many may go
on — sealed behind a door, left to hold a line, given up to a siege — then it
is your duty to spend them, and to bear it. Sentiment that risks the whole for
the part is a luxury endurance cannot afford.
